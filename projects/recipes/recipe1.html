Background

Everyday for the past year, I have been waking up and preparing the same boring breakfast for myself before I head out for school, work, or exercise. One Saturday morning, I came to a realization- this is not the life that I was destined to live. Thus, I thought to myself, "what would I enjoy eating right now?" Among the many delectable options, there were a few that stood out, each with their own complications:

  1. Cereal. This wouldn't work; too much milk to sit right in my stomach, not enough opportunity for creativity, and far less nutrients than desired.
  2. Eggs and bacon. This was too boring; I wouldn't enjoy every bite like I want to be.
  3. Doughnuts. Ah, what I would do for a doughnut and coffee for my breakfast, while sitting on a balcony, staring at the sunrise. Unfortunately, I live in America, and not Venice, so there's not much to stare at outside other than industrialization, and I can't get away with having a doughnut every morning without my body hating me.

So what was I going to make?

Protein Pancakes!

Why, you might ask?

  • They are super easy to prepare and make (10-15 minutes total)
  • They are very fun to eat, with lots of bursting flavors due to the diverse, harmonious ingredients used
  • They are packed with essential macronutrients and micronutrients, which are essential to kickstart your day with a smile

When we first saw him preparing these pancakes, we were sure that the taste would be hindered by the addition of protein powder into the mix. But, after we tried them, we could then proudly exclaim that these are even better than restraurant pancakes!

The Recipe

Ingredients
Calories: 800 | Protein: 90g | Carbs: 74g | Fat: 26g
Ingredients Instructions
  • 150g egg whites
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 80g oats
  • 2 scoops protein powder
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter
  1. In a blender, add all of the ingredients and blend until there is a smooth liquid structure.
  2. Preheat a pan over a stovetop with some cooking oil until it is hot.
  3. Add the pancake mix until it covers the area of the pan, and wait until "bubbles" begin to form on the top layer of the pancake mix.
  4. Once the bubbles have formed, gently pick up the pancake to check the bottomside and see if its ready to flip.
  5. If ready, gently flip the pancake. Some leakage of the pancake mix onto the pan is expected during the flip, but that's perfectly fine.
  6. Give a few minutes for the other side of the pancake to solidify, then take the pancake off the pan and onto a plate.
  7. Add your favorite toppings onto the pancake (e.g. bananas, blueberries, chocolate chips, maple syrup).
  8. Repeat steps until you have produced a desirable amount of pancakes. Or, save the pancake mix in the fridge for later! Enjoy!
Preparation Time: 10-15 minutes | Servings: 5 large pancakes

Tips & Tricks

  • Use non-stick pans to avoid sticking and ensure even cooking.
  • Replace peanut butter with almond butter or a nut-free spread if you wish. The main purpose of the peanut butter in this recipe is not its taste, but the texture balance that it provides.
  • Experiment with different-flavored protein powders and choose your accompanying toppings accordingly. For example, the protein powder I used for this recipe was Ghost's Cinnabon flavor protein powder.
